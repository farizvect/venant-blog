The software industry is shifting. The traditional path of hiring junior developers to write boilerplate, fix simple bugs, and manage minor deployments is obsolete. Agentic AI now handles these tasks with greater speed and zero onboarding overhead.

A human junior requires months of training, mentorship, and code reviews. An agent requires clear instructions and context. Once configured, it executes. It scaffolds projects, debugs errors, and pushes deployments autonomously.

This is not about replacing humans; it is about raising the baseline. The new entry-level requirement is no longer writing syntax. It is system architecture, orchestration, and managing AI agents. 

Small teams and solo operators can now scale their output to match large organizations. The bottleneck is no longer hands on keyboards. It is clarity of thought.
