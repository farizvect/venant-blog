Entering the IT industry no longer guarantees a comfort zone. Low-level IT jobs—boilerplate coders, basic sysadmins, L1/L2 support, and manual QA—are heavily commoditized by AI. The value of an IT professional has shifted from typing speed to the ability to design systems, secure architecture, and orchestrate AI agents.

Tech companies aren't executing mass layoffs because they're going bankrupt. They are reallocating capital. Headcounts are slashed to buy GPUs and build AI infrastructure.

Data from 2024 to 2026:

**Regional:**
- **GoTo:** Cut workforce from 7.522 to 3.352.
- **TikTok Shop / Tokopedia:** Redundant staff post-merger.
- **Traveloka:** Reorganized with a focus on AI and engineering.
- **Livspace:** Reduced 1.000 roles for automated systems.

**Global:**
- **Amazon:** Cut 30.000 roles, injected $100 Billion into AI data centers.
- **Meta:** Removed 16.000 roles, invested $115 Billion in AI R&D.
- **Oracle:** Eliminated 30.000 jobs to expand GPU data centers.

Companies invest in silicon and algorithms, not human bureaucracy.

Newcomers face a binary choice: pivot out of IT, or adopt the "Mahoraga" mentality. Mahoraga is a mythological entity that adapts to any attack. Engineers need this survival mentality.

The barrier to entry for software creation is near zero. Anyone can use AI agents to generate scripts or smart contracts. Newcomers compete with AI agents and thousands of laid-off senior engineers flooding the market.

Two strongholds remain difficult for AI to breach:

**System Security**
Code production speed directly correlates with bug production. In the Web3 ecosystem, AI writes smart contracts 10x faster and introduces vulnerabilities 10x faster. A small logic flaw can drain millions from a liquidity pool. AI scans for known vulnerabilities but lacks the human creativity to find zero-day exploits. Security Auditors and Security Engineers hold the power.

**New Value Creation**
Generative AI is an interpolation engine. It ingests historical data and spits it back out. AI struggles with extrapolation—creating 100% original paradigms. Content creation and innovative product design belong to humans. Humans who build new solutions secure their positions.

Future IT workers are directors, not typists. Our job is to architect systems, audit security, and inject originality. Adapt or be replaced by machines.
